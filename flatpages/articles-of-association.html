Articles of Association

Association: Agora

of September 10, 2025

Section I - Name, Seat, Duration and Purpose of the Association

Article 1: Name, Seat, Duration

Under the name "Agora", there exists an association in accordance with the provisions of the Swiss Civil Code, with its seat in the city of Lucerne (the Association).

The duration of the Association is unlimited.

Article 2: Purpose

The Association is a non-profit organization dedicated to fostering trust on the internet, ensuring it remains a global public resource for all.

To this end, the Association shall promote the creation, standardization, and public adoption of open, secure, and user-centric technologies. This may include developing software, creating educational resources, organizing events, and engaging in any other activities that serve the Association's purpose.

Section II - Membership

Article 3: Membership, Admission, Register of Members

Members of the Association may be Swiss and foreign natural and legal persons, including public law institutions and corporations.

The application for admission as a member of the Association must be addressed to the Board in the form determined by the Board, which shall decide finally on the admission at its discretion. The Board may refuse the application without stating any reasons.

The Board shall maintain a register of members in which the members' names, addresses, and e-mail addresses are entered. In relation to the Association, only those persons who are entered in the register of members shall be considered members.

If a person entered in the register of members changes their name, address, or e-mail address, they must notify the Board of this change. Communications from the Association shall be deemed to have been validly made if they are sent to the contact information recorded in the register of members.

Article 4: Resignation, Expulsion, Suspension

Membership shall terminate upon resignation, expulsion, and death (in the case of natural persons) or loss of legal personality (in the case of legal persons).

Resignation from the Association is possible with three months' notice to the end of a month. The notice of resignation must be sent to the Board in writing or in another form determined by the Board. A resignation does not affect the obligation to pay the membership fee for the current fiscal year.

The Board may definitively expel a member from the Association who fails to pay the membership fee in due time even after a written reminder. Instead of expulsion, the Board may also order a suspension of membership (including voting rights) until the outstanding amount has been paid in full.

Furthermore, the Board may expel a member who, in the Board's opinion, acts contrary to the interests of the Association. In this case, the expulsion shall be effected by a resolution passed by a majority of two-thirds of all Board members and by written notification to the member concerned, stating the reason for the expulsion.

Before making a decision on expulsion, the Board shall grant the member concerned the right to be heard.

The expelled member may appeal against a decision of expulsion by the Board pursuant to paragraph 4 to the next General Assembly within 30 calendar days from the date of the written notification. The appeal must be submitted to the Board in writing. The member concerned shall be suspended (including voting rights) until the next General Assembly. The General Assembly shall decide definitively on the approval of the appeal by the usual majority. If the appeal is approved, the membership of the expelled member is reinstated, and the member concerned cannot be expelled again for the same reason for one year from the date of the decision of the General Assembly.

Section III - Organs

Article 5: Organs

The organs of the Association are:

  • the General Assembly;
  • the Board; and
  • the Auditors.

A. The General Assembly

Article 6: Powers of the General Assembly

The supreme organ of the Association is the General Assembly of the members. It has the following exclusive powers:

  • the adoption and amendment of these Bylaws;
  • the election and removal of the members of the Board and the Auditors;
  • the approval of the annual financial statements;
  • the discharge of the members of the Board and of the persons entrusted with the management;
  • decisions on appeals against expulsion resolutions of the Board;
  • the resolution on the dissolution of the Association;
  • the determination of the membership fees;
  • the passing of resolutions on matters reserved to the General Assembly by law or by these Bylaws or submitted to it by the Board.

Article 7: Ordinary and Extraordinary General Assembly, Conduct

The ordinary General Assembly shall, in principle, be held annually within six months of the close of the Association's fiscal year.

Extraordinary General Assemblies shall be held if:

  • the Board or the Auditors deem it appropriate;
  • a General Assembly so resolves; or
  • at least 20 percent of all members jointly request it in writing or in another form determined by the Board, stating the item to be discussed and the proposal, and in the case of elections, the names of the proposed candidates.

General Assemblies may be held physically, virtually, or in a hybrid form. The Board shall determine the details.

Article 8: Convening

The General Assembly shall be convened by the Board or, if necessary, by the Auditors, at the latest 30 calendar days before the day of the meeting. The Board may delegate the right of convocation by means of organizational regulations to individual Board members or a management body.

The notice of convocation must contain the provisional agenda items (agenda) as well as the proposals of the Board and of the member or members who have requested the holding of a General Assembly and the inclusion of an item on the agenda in accordance with Article 9 paragraph 2 of these Bylaws, and in the case of elections, the names of the proposed candidates.

No later than 10 calendar days before the day of the meeting, the convening body shall provide the members with the binding agenda with the definitive items for discussion as well as the proposals of the Board and of the member or members who have requested the inclusion of an item on the agenda in accordance with Article 11 paragraph 1 of these Bylaws, and in the case of elections, the names of the proposed candidates.

No later than 30 calendar days before the ordinary General Assembly, the annual report and any audit report shall be made available to the members for inspection. The Board shall determine the details. The members shall be informed thereof in the notice of convocation.

The convocation to the General Assembly and the delivery of the binding agenda shall be made in accordance with Article 30 of these Bylaws.

Article 9: Agenda Setting

At least 20 percent of all members may jointly demand that an item be placed on the agenda. The request must be made in writing or in another form determined by the Board at least 20 calendar days before the day of the meeting, stating the agenda item and the members' proposals, and in the case of elections, the names of the proposed candidates.

Subject to Article 11 of these Bylaws, the General Assembly cannot pass resolutions on proposals concerning items not duly announced; exceptions are motions made at a General Assembly to convene an extraordinary General Assembly and to elect Auditors following a request by a member, if no Auditors have yet been elected.

No prior notice is required for the submission of motions within the scope of the agenda items and for discussions without a resolution.

Article 10: Universal Assembly

If no objection is raised, all members may hold a General Assembly without observing the formal requirements for convening it. At this meeting, all matters falling within the scope of business of the General Assembly can be validly discussed and resolved, as long as all members are present and the Board members have been invited and are either present or have waived their right to participate.

Article 11: Chair, Tellers, Minutes

The President of the Board shall chair the General Assembly. In his or her absence, the Vice President of the Board, another member, or a person designated by the Board shall preside. If no member of the Board is available and the Board has not appointed a representative, the chairperson shall be elected by the General Assembly.

The chairperson of the General Assembly shall appoint a secretary and the tellers, all of whom need not be members. The minutes shall be signed by the chairperson and the secretary.

The chairperson of the General Assembly has all the powers of direction that are conducive to the proper conduct of the General Assembly.

Any member may make procedural motions during the General Assembly. The General Assembly shall decide on procedural questions by immediate resolution; in doing so, it may deviate from the chairperson's instructions.

Article 12: Voting Rights, Representation of Members

Each member has one vote.

A member may be represented at the General Assembly by its legal representative or, by means of a power of attorney, by another authorized person who must also be a member of the Association. However, the Board may also admit third parties who are not members of the Association as authorized persons. If the member is a legal entity, the above restriction does not apply to its corporate bodies, employees, and other permanent representatives.

The Board may issue rules of procedure regarding participation and representation at the General Assembly and shall regulate the requirements for powers of attorney and instructions.

Article 13: Resolutions, Elections

All duly convened General Assemblies shall have a quorum irrespective of the number of members present or validly represented.

The General Assembly passes resolutions and elects by an absolute majority of the valid votes cast (excluding abstentions), unless the law or these Bylaws provide otherwise.

If an election is not successful in the first ballot and there is more than one candidate, the chairperson of the General Assembly shall order a second ballot in which the relative majority shall decide.

Voting and elections shall be held by open ballot, unless the General Assembly or its chairperson decides that they shall be held by secret ballot.

The chairperson of the General Assembly may at any time have a vote or election repeated if, in his or her opinion, there is doubt as to the result of the vote; in this case, the previous vote or election shall be deemed not to have taken place.

Article 14: Important Resolutions

A resolution of the General Assembly passed by at least two-thirds of the valid votes cast (excluding abstentions) is required for:

  • the amendment of the Association's purpose;
  • the dissolution of the Association;
  • the removal of Board members.

Provisions of the Bylaws that stipulate greater majorities for the passing of certain resolutions than those prescribed by law may only be introduced or repealed with the required majority.

Article 15: Circular Resolution

If the Board orders a circular resolution, the written or electronic approval of the applicable majority of the members who validly respond (excluding abstentions) to a proposal shall be equivalent to a resolution of the General Assembly.

B. The Board

Article 16: Members of the Board

The Board shall consist of at least three and at most seven members.

Any natural person with legal capacity who is a member of the Association, as well as corporate bodies, employees, or other permanent representatives of a member of the Association, may be elected to the Board.

The members of the Board shall, in principle, serve in an honorary capacity. The Board may regulate the reimbursement of expenses and the conclusion of insurance policies for the benefit of its members.

All members of the Board shall disclose their interests in an appropriate form; candidates proposed for election shall disclose their interests prior to the election.

Article 17: Election and Term of Office

The Board members appointed by the founding members are elected for a fixed term of three years, calculated from the date of the Association's foundation. Thereafter, ordinary elections for members of the Board shall be held by the General Assembly every three years for a term of office of three years.

Board members who are elected to the Board by the General Assembly on an extraordinary basis, i.e., on a date not coinciding with an ordinary election date, shall only be elected for the remainder of the term of office until the next ordinary election date.

The General Assembly may remove members of the Board at any time. Any claims for compensation by the removed members shall remain unaffected.

Article 18: Organization

The Board shall constitute itself. It shall appoint from among its members its President, as well as one or more Vice Presidents and the delegate, as required.

For important reasons, a Board member may be suspended from his or her function by a resolution of the Board with a majority of two-thirds of all Board members (excluding the one concerned). In this case, the Board shall immediately convene an extraordinary General Assembly for which the removal of the suspended Board member is placed on the agenda. If the General Assembly rejects the removal, the suspension shall lapse without further action.

The Board may otherwise regulate its organization and decision-making by means of organizational regulations.

Article 19: Convening, Resolutions, Minutes

Meetings of the Board shall be convened by the President or, in his or her absence, by the Vice President or another member of the Board, as often as deemed necessary, but at least twice per fiscal year, or when a Board member so requests, stating the reasons.

Board meetings may be held physically, virtually, or in a hybrid form. The Board shall determine the details, unless the organizational regulations provide otherwise.

Unless the organizational regulations issued by the Board provide otherwise, the presence of a majority of the Board members is required for a quorum.

Unless the organizational regulations issued by the Board provide otherwise, the Board shall pass its resolutions by a majority of the votes cast, and the chairperson shall have the casting vote.

The resolutions shall be recorded in minutes, which shall be signed by the chairperson and the secretary.

Resolutions may also be passed by written circular, provided that no member requests oral deliberation.

Article 20: Powers of the Board

The Board may pass resolutions on all matters that are not assigned to another organ of the Association by law, these Bylaws, or a set of regulations.

It has, among others, the following duties:

  • the overall management of the Association and the issuing of the necessary directives
  • the determination of the organization of the Association;
  • the organization of the accounting system;
  • the appointment and removal of the persons entrusted with the management and representation of the Association and the regulation of the signing authority;
  • the ultimate supervision of the persons entrusted with the management, in particular with regard to compliance with the law, these Bylaws, the regulations, and directives;
  • the preparation of the annual report as well as the preparation of the General Assembly and the execution of its resolutions;
  • other tasks and powers reserved to the Board by law or these Bylaws.

C. The Auditors

Article 21: Auditors and Audit

The General Assembly shall elect the Auditors for a term of office until the conclusion of the next ordinary General Assembly. Re-election is possible.

The General Assembly may remove the Auditors at any time. Any claims for compensation by the Auditors shall remain unaffected.

The Auditors must meet the statutory requirements. The Auditors shall have the powers and duties assigned to them by law.

The Board may at any time instruct the Auditors to carry out special investigations, in particular interim audits, and to report thereon.

The General Assembly may expressly or tacitly waive the election of Auditors if the Association is not obliged to have an ordinary audit or a limited statutory audit. This waiver shall also apply to subsequent years.

Section IV - Financing, Liability and Fiscal Year

Article 22: Financing of the Association, Liability

The resources of the Association for the pursuit of its purpose consist of:

  • membership fees;
  • voluntary contributions from members or third parties (including donations, legacies, grants, sponsorship funds, etc.);
  • income from the Association's activities and assets;
  • dividends and other distributions from subsidiaries.

The funds may be used exclusively for the promotion of the Association's purpose. A distribution of funds to members (through dividends or similar transactions) is expressly excluded.

Only the Association's assets shall be liable for the Association's debts. Any personal liability of the members is excluded.

Article 23: Membership Fee

The General Assembly, upon proposal of the Board, determines the membership fee for each fiscal year.

The Board may propose different levels of contribution. It shall determine the criteria according to which members are assigned to a contribution level and shall publish them or communicate them prior to joining.

In justified cases, the Board may exempt a member from paying the membership fee.

The Board shall communicate the membership fee for the following year at the latest four months before the end of the current fiscal year.

Article 24: Fiscal Year, Annual Report

The fiscal year of the Association ends on December 31, unless the Board sets a different date.

The Board shall prepare an annual report for each fiscal year with annual financial statements (consisting of at least an income statement and a balance sheet).

Section V - Dissolution, Liquidation

Article 25: Dissolution, Liquidation

The General Assembly may at any time resolve to dissolve and liquidate the Association in accordance with the statutory and bylaw provisions.

The liquidation shall be carried out by the Board, unless it is entrusted to other persons by the General Assembly.

The liquidation of the Association shall be carried out in accordance with the statutory provisions. The liquidators are authorized to sell assets (including real estate) by private contract.

After payment of the Association's debts, the assets shall be transferred to one or more non-profit legal entities with their seat in Switzerland, designated by the General Assembly, which pursue a similar objective to the Association.

Section VI - Applicable Law, Dispute Resolution

Article 26: Applicable Law

These Bylaws, any further regulations and decrees of the Association as well as legal relationships between the Association and its members shall be governed exclusively by Swiss substantive law, to the exclusion of the rules of private international law.

Article 27: Dispute Resolution

All disputes, disagreements or claims

  • in matters of the Association;
  • between the Association and its members; and
  • arising out of or in connection with these Bylaws or any other regulations or decrees of the Association shall be decided by arbitration in accordance with the Swiss Rules of International Arbitration of the Swiss Arbitration Centre. The version of the Rules in force at the time the notice of arbitration is submitted shall apply.

The arbitral tribunal shall consist of three members.

The seat of the arbitration shall be Lucerne, Switzerland.

The language of the arbitration shall be English, unless the parties agree on German as the language of the arbitration, whereby evidence in its original German, French or Italian language may also be submitted without translation.

The provisions of the 12th chapter of the Federal Act on Private International Law shall not apply; instead, the provisions of the third part of the Swiss Civil Procedure Code shall apply.

Admission to the Association is conditional upon express acceptance of this arbitration clause.

Section VII - Miscellaneous

Article 28: Notices

Written communications from the Association shall be sent by letter to the last address of the member entered in the register of members. If neither the law nor these Bylaws require written notification, the Association may also send notifications to the last e-mail address of the member entered in the register of members or in another suitable manner.

Where the law or these Bylaws provide for notices from members to the Association to be in writing, the notices must be sent by registered mail or internationally recognized courier to the official address of the Association and be signed by hand.

Article 29: Entry into Force

These Bylaws were adopted at the founding meeting of September 10, 2025 and entered into force on that date.

Lucerne, September 10, 2025
